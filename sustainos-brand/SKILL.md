# SustainOS Brand Identity

## Who This Brand Is

SustainOS helps sustainability professionals build their own AI-powered operating system, so they can do more of the work that actually matters.

It started with one selfish question: "How can I use AI to do real work as a sustainability manager, not the hype?" That question turned into SustainOS.

The credibility is built by doing. Zyad Hatquai is a chemical engineer and sustainability manager who learned AI by applying it to his own real work first. He tested regulation research, built audit companions, compared documents against standards, and built skills, guides, tools, dashboards, and assessment apps. He did not study AI in the abstract and then teach it. He did the work, saw what held up, and shares that.

The brand sounds like a practitioner sharing what they learned, never an expert lecturing from above. As Zyad puts it: "Not the smartest in the room when it comes to AI. Not the deepest expert in sustainability either. But I'm curious. I test things. I apply. And I share what actually works."

SustainOS offers a cohort program (AILab, focused on moving from chatting with AI to executing with AI), 1:1 coaching (workflow audits, building workflows, moving from overwhelmed to executing), and digital products (skills, guides, tools). Current focus is B2C.

## The Core Promise

Your domain knowledge is the product. The skill gap is not coding. It is knowing what problem to solve, and the audience already has that. AI and the right workflow turn that knowledge into tools, dashboards, and systems, with no developer, no retainer, near-zero cost.

Recurring framing the brand returns to constantly:
- "Your knowledge is the gold. Your intellectual property."
- "Move from chatting with AI to executing with AI."
- "You are not being replaced. You are being upgraded."
- "I don't want to convince you to use AI. I want to show you what is possible."
- "AI gave everyone the same tools. It did not give everyone the same expertise."
- "Garbage in, garbage out still applies."

## Audience

The primary audience is ambitious sustainability professionals: in-house sustainability managers, freelance sustainability consultants, and people building their own consulting practice, service business, or brand.

They already believe in AI. They do not need convincing. What defines them is a specific tension: they understand the opportunity, which is exactly why they feel overwhelmed and behind. They are stuck in chat mode and have not moved to building. They feel FOMO because they are paying attention when most people are not.

What they need from SustainOS: to see what is actually possible, see what has already been built, and get a concrete path to execute themselves.

They do not code, and they should never be made to feel they need to. Their expertise (CSRD, SBTi, EU Taxonomy, materiality, audits, supplier engagement, the business know-how) is the asset that matters.

How the brand treats them: as capable, expert people who simply have not had the right tools or the right bridge to AI built for them yet. Never as people who need to be educated about their own field.

## Color System

| Role | Name | Hex | Usage rule |
|---|---|---|---|
| Brand primary | Purple | `#6E6CFF` | Headers, accent labels, key highlighted words in body copy, full-bleed backgrounds for LinkedIn graphics and carousels. The signature SustainOS color. |
| Secondary accent | Orange | `#D4622A` | Headline accent words on dark thumbnails, website accents, secondary emphasis. Pairs with charcoal. |
| Highlight | Lime | `#C8F135` | Pills and tags, callouts, buttons and CTAs, key terms inside dark layouts. Use sparingly. |
| Dark background | Charcoal | `#2B2B2B` | Dark-mode YouTube thumbnails, dark carousels and one-pagers. |
| Light background | Off-white | `#F5F4EF` | Light carousels, documents, light slides. |
| Neutral / contrast | White | `#FFFFFF` | Floating cards over colored backgrounds, content surfaces inside dark layouts. |

Two visual modes, both valid: dark mode (charcoal background, orange accent) or light/purple mode (purple or off-white background). Pick one per piece. Do not mix both in a single graphic.

Text color rule: charcoal `#2B2B2B` for body text on light backgrounds, white on dark or purple backgrounds.

## Typography System

| Use case | Font | Weight | Rule |
|---|---|---|---|
| Headlines, hooks, titles | Poppins | ExtraBold or Black (800-900) | Heavy and confident. All-caps common for thumbnails. |
| Subheads | Poppins | SemiBold (600) | One step down from headline. |
| Body text | Poppins | Regular (400) or Medium (500) | Clean and readable. |
| Accent / label text | Poppins | SemiBold or Bold | For pills, tags, small labels. |

Poppins is the only typeface. Do not introduce a second font.

## Visual Style Rules

What SustainOS visuals always do:
- Use real screenshots of real tools. Show the thing actually working.
- Use specific numbers, real tool names, and concrete outcomes.
- Carry a signature: `SustainOS` and/or `@ZyadHatquai`, usually bottom corner.
- Stay clean and readable even when information-dense.

The "never" list:
- No stock photography.
- No green leaves, globes, planet imagery, recycling symbols, or generic eco visuals.
- No SDG color wheel as decoration.
- No generic corporate-sustainability aesthetic.
- No abstract shapes that decorate without explaining.
- No second font, no off-palette colors.
- Never both color modes mixed in one graphic.

## Voice and Tone

Voice adjectives: conversational, peer-to-peer, enthusiastic, plain-language but technically precise.

Stance: practitioner, never expert-from-above. The brand shares what was tested and what worked. Honest, occasionally self-deprecating, never the ultimate authority.

Technical precision: plain language, but use the correct terms (skill, .md file, artifact, connector, Project, Scope 3, CSRD, SBTi) without dumbing down. Never over-explain sustainability concepts to sustainability professionals.

Writing mechanics:
- Short sentences. Generous line breaks. Lots of white space.
- One clear point per piece. Two at most. One is better.
- Lead with the reader's real situation or a concrete scene.
- Close with a CTA that grows naturally from the point just made.

Punctuation rule: the em dash (—) may be used as a structural separator between a label and its description ("Newsletter — structured and valuable"). It must not be used as a mid-sentence punctuation break inside flowing prose. Use commas, periods, or parentheses instead.

Always sounds like:
- "Let me be honest with you."
- "Move from chatting with AI to executing with AI."
- "Your knowledge is the gold."
- "I test things. I apply. And I share what actually works."
- "You don't need to know how to code. You need to know what you want built."

Never sounds like:
- Hype or magic-button language.
- Expert-lecturing-down tone.
- Generic AI-for-everyone advice. SustainOS content is built specifically for sustainability work.
- Fear-mongering. The brand reframes "you are behind" into "you have a head start because you are paying attention."

## Format-Specific Guidance

**LinkedIn** — Load `linkedin/linkedin.md` before writing any LinkedIn post. That file contains the full post structure, CTA transition rules, and the interactive CTA checklist to run at the end of every draft.

**Newsletter** — More structured than LinkedIn. A genuinely valuable resource the reader would not want to skip: clear insights, step-by-step instructions, things figured out while building. Must lead to action. Usually anchors the bi-weekly video and points to AILab.

**YouTube** — Scripted, enthusiastic, walks through a real scenario (often using a fictional company such as "The Corporate"). Honest about what AI does not do: the human provides judgment, targets, strategy, and context. AI executes the packaging. Strong hook in the first five seconds.

## Applying This Brand

### For visual outputs (apps, dashboards, graphics, thumbnails, slides):
1. Pick one mode: dark (charcoal + orange) or light/purple. Do not mix.
2. Set type in Poppins. Headline in ExtraBold/Black, body in Regular/Medium.
3. Apply the palette by role: purple for headers and key accents, lime for highlights and CTAs, orange for thumbnail accents, charcoal or off-white for backgrounds.
4. Use real screenshots, real numbers, real tool names. No stock imagery, no eco clichés.
5. Add the SustainOS signature.
6. Check the "never" list before finishing.

### For written outputs (posts, scripts, newsletters, captions, titles):
1. Lead with the reader's real situation or a concrete scene.
2. Make one clear point. Cut the second one if there is one.
3. Short sentences, generous line breaks, plain language with precise terms.
4. Practitioner voice: share what was tested, stay honest, no hype, no lecturing.
5. No mid-sentence em dashes. Em dash only as a label separator.
6. Close with a CTA that transitions naturally from the post's main point.
7. Reinforce a core line where it fits naturally.

### For LinkedIn posts specifically:
Load `linkedin/linkedin.md` before writing. Follow the post structure, CTA transition rules, and end-of-draft checklist defined there.
